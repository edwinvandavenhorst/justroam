Sun and Island in the Autumn Break: Elba

A quick autumn getaway to test the truck's new interior: sun, an island, and as little driving at the destination as possible.

At a glance

  • Why we went: After Bosnia, the truck's interior needed a rework: with version 2.0 installed and a school holiday coming up, this trip doubled as the shakedown test.
  • Best moment: Finding the perfect secluded camping spot on Elba, right by the water and out of the wind: days of hiking, eating at the camp, and the beach whenever we wanted.
  • Hardest part: A brutal, unplanned freezing night in northern Italy: ice on the windows by morning after temperatures dropped far more than forecast.
  • Would we do it again: Yes: the kids already want to go back, especially to the boutique hotel in France where the trip ended.

The route vibe

After the Bosnia trip we knew the truck's interior needed changes. With version 2.0 installed and a school holiday coming up, a trip was the obvious way to test it out. Edwin was clear on the brief: sun, an island, and as little driving as possible once we arrived. Corsica didn't quite work out logistically, and then Elba turned up in the search: none of us knew much more about it than being the island Napoleon was exiled to, but good Italian food and good weather sealed the decision.

To dodge bad weather in Austria we pushed straight through to northern Italy: 1000+ km in one day, saved by finding a spot with a late check-in. The temperature had been 20°C+ all day, but it dropped with every kilometre, and by the time we parked it was 12°C and falling. Our sleeping bags were rated for it on paper, but the temperature kept dropping overnight until there was ice on the windows by morning, genuinely one of our worst nights. A warm shower and hot drinks got the mood back before heading for the ferry.

A 1.5-hour ferry crossing later, we were on Elba. We picked our camping spot straight off the ferry and got lucky: close to the water, secluded, sheltered from the wind. It became home for the next few days: hiking from camp, eating at camp, the beach whenever we felt like it. Maximum relaxation. But two separate calls from friends offering us a place to stay changed the plan, and we left Elba midweek. The drive through the Italian/French Alps on the way out was stunning, enough that it's already noted down as its own future trip.

After a stop for the night and a great dinner with friends, we finished the trip at a boutique hotel, [ABC], in the north of France. As good as the overlanding and the Elba camping had been, nothing quite beat a proper bed and an evening of stories with friends until the early hours. The kids loved it enough that they're already talking about going back to help run the hotel.

Highlights

Practical notes

Real notes from the trip, not exhaustive, but the things that actually mattered on the road.

  • Weather swing: Don't trust a single day's forecast on a long push: we went from 20°C+ to a below-freezing, ice-on-the-windows night in one drive.
  • Sleeping bags: Rated comfort temperatures didn't match reality: pack for colder than the label says if you're crossing mountain regions.
  • Ferry to Elba: About 1.5 hours.
  • Interior test: This trip doubled as the shakedown for the truck's reworked (v2.0) interior after lessons learned in Bosnia.

What we'd do differently next time

  • Pack warmer sleeping gear even on a "sun and island" trip if the route crosses mountains: the cold snap in northern Italy caught us off guard.
  • Build in less of a rushed, single-day 1000+ km push if it can be avoided: a late check-in bailed us out, but it was tight.
  • Come back and actually do the Italian/French Alps as their own trip, not just a transit route.
